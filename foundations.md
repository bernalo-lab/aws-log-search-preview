# Foundations

Core principles and mental models for effective log analysis. This section explains how experienced engineers approach logs as investigative signals rather than answers, helping you interpret noisy systems and apply the recipes more effectively.

---

## Foundations

Before jumping into individual recipes, it’s worth aligning on how experienced engineers approach log searching.

Logs are not answers.  
They are clues.

Effective log analysis is less about syntax and more about:

- Asking the right question
- Narrowing uncertainty
- Preserving context
- Understanding system behaviour over time

This section introduces the mental models and patterns that make the recipes effective.

---

## How to use this section

You don’t need to memorise these ideas.

Read this section once, then return to it when:

- Searches feel unproductive
- Logs feel noisy
- Everything looks broken at once

The recipes assume this way of thinking.
